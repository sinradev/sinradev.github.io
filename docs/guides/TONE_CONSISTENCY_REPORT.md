---

## Brand Voice Baseline: "Pragmatisme Élégant"

### Definition
A tone that is:
- **Confident without aggression**  -  Solutions-oriented, not problem-focused
- **Professional but accessible**  -  Uses sophisticated vocabulary without becoming stiff
- **Operationally-grounded**  -  Emphasizes real-world results and measurable outcomes
- **Implicit rather than explicit**  -  Shows confidence through substance, not hype
- **B2B SaaS**  -  Targets experienced technical teams and decision-makers

### Reference Style Guides
- ✅ Similar to: Notion, Linear, GitLab (sophisticated, direct, results-focused)
- ❌ Not like: Slack (too cool/casual), Atlassian (too corporate), Figma (too disruptive)

### Key Characteristics
- Avoid: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "disrupt," "kill," punchy exclamations
- Embrace: Operational clarity, specific benefits, confidence through substance
- Tone markers: Direct statements, comparative frameworks, focus on team outcomes

## Key Strengths

### 1. Operational Grounding
Content consistently starts with *real problems* teams face:
- Scattered documentation, QA delays, unclear priorities
- Context-switching, overallocation, invisible capacity
- Examples are specific and believable

### 2. Solution-Focused Narrative
Rather than problem-hammering, content shows *how Sinra addresses* each challenge:
- Feature descriptions include operational context
- Case studies show implementation, not just benefits
- Language emphasizes control and visibility, not transformation

### 3. Confidence Through Substance
The brand doesn't oversell:
- Avoids "revolutionary," "game-changing," "disruption"
- Positions as "the tool built for hybrid methodologies" (specific, not superlative)
- Results are specific (40%, 85%, 6 weeks) not inflated (100x faster, eliminate meetings)

### 4. Technical Credibility
Content uses appropriate technical vocabulary:
- Kanban, Hotwire, polymorphic comments, stage gates, acceptance criteria
- Shows decision-makers the platform understands their complexity
- Positions against real competitors (Jira, Azure DevOps, Asana)

## Areas for Improvement

### 1. Testimonials  -  Minor Language Refinement

**Current Issue:**
- One testimonial uses "game-changer" which conflicts with "avoid hype" guideline
- Antoine P.'s quote: "The dual workflow approach in Sinra has been a game-changer for our team"

**Recommendation:**
Replace with operationally-grounded alternative:
> "The dual workflow approach in Sinra eliminated the bottlenecks we used to face. Product teams and developers now work in parallel seamlessly, instead of blocking each other."

---

### 2. Pricing Page  -  Add Value Narrative

**Current Issue:**
- Pricing page is feature-list only; no narrative connecting price to value
- Lacks Pragmatisme Élégant positioning

**Recommendation:**
Add brief intro paragraph (before pricing tiers) such as:

> "Sinra pricing is straightforward: pay per seat, no hidden fees. Whether you're scaling a startup or managing enterprise complexity, Sinra adapts to your team size and grows with you. Start with a 14-day free trial - no credit card required."

This maintains professional tone while emphasizing flexibility and operational reality.

---

### 3. Contact/FAQ  -  Enhance Brand Personality

**Current Issue:**

**Recommendation:**
Enhance answers with brief operational context:

> "Our support team typically responds to all inquiries within 24 hours during business days. Because we've worked with teams in regulated industries, we understand when urgent issues arise - call us directly for immediate assistance."

This adds personality without sacrificing professionalism.

## Future Content Guidelines

When creating new content (blog posts, case studies, feature announcements, marketing copy):

**DO:**
- Start with operational reality (real problems teams face)
- Provide frameworks and decision-making tools
- Use specific metrics and measurable outcomes
- Show, don't tell (examples over claims)
- Use technical vocabulary appropriately
- Maintain confidence without hype

**DON'T:**
- Use hyperbolic language (revolutionary, disruptive, game-changing, kill)
- Focus on problems without showing solutions
- Make unsubstantiated claims
- Adopt casual or cool tone (save for social media)
- Oversimplify complex methodological choices
- Use generic praise (amazing, incredible, best)
